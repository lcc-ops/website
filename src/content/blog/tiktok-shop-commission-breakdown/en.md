TikTok Shop's fee structure is the shortest among major marketplaces, but it punishes sellers who do not model the creator affiliate cut. Here is the real cost on a $30 product.

## Fee stack on a $30 product

| Fee | Amount | % of price |
|---|---|---|
| **Sale price** | **$30.00** | **100%** |
| Referral commission (6%, fashion) | $1.80 | 6% |
| Transaction fee (1.8%) | $0.54 | 1.8% |
| **TikTok's cut** | **$2.34** | **7.8%** |
| Creator affiliate (10% typical) | $3.00 | 10% |
| TikTok's cut of creator fee (30%) | $0.90 | 3% |
| **Creator + TikTok affiliate layer** | **$3.90** | **13%** |
| **Total platform + creator** | **$6.24** | **20.8%** |

That is the floor. Add a 5% return rate, 10% offsite ad spend, and a $0.30 fixed transaction cost, and the picture changes.

Compared to Amazon's 15% referral, TikTok Shop's commission is one-third. But the creator affiliate layer is what makes the math unforgiving if you skip the modeling.

## Creator affiliate — the hidden cost

Most TikTok Shop sellers rely on creators. You set the commission rate (5–20% is typical), and any creator who posts your product earns that percentage on every sale they drive.

When a creator drives a sale:

- Creator earns: rate × price (e.g., 10% × $30 = $3.00)
- TikTok earns: 30% of what the creator earns (~$0.90 on a $3.00 commission)

A 10% creator commission effectively costs you 13% of revenue, not 10%.

## Strategy: how to model affiliate cost

Treat affiliate as a marketing line, not a fee line.

```
net_margin = gross_margin − referral − transaction − return_rate × return_cost − ad_spend
```

For a $30 product, $9 cost, 10% affiliate, 8% return rate, 15% ad spend:

```
gross_margin = (30 − 9 − 2.34) / 30 = 0.622 (62.2%)
return_cost = 4.00 (return shipping)
return_loss = 0.08 × 4.00 = 0.32
ad_spend = 0.15 × 30 = 4.50
affiliate_cost = 0.13 × 30 = 3.90

net = 30 − 9 − 2.34 − 0.32 − 4.50 − 3.90
net = 9.94
net_margin = 33.1%
```

Still healthy, but only if creators convert at 4% or better. Below that, the affiliate layer turns your 60%+ gross into a single-digit net.

## Return window and cost

TikTok Shop has a 90-day return window. Most categories require sellers to pay return shipping:

- Standard shipping label: $3.00–$4.50
- For oversized items: $6.00–$9.00

A 10% return rate on a $30 product with $4 return shipping costs $1.20 per sale on average, before any restocking.

## New-seller incentives

TikTok Shop runs a 90-day new seller program:

- 0% referral commission for 90 days (categories vary)
- 0% transaction fee for 30 days
- Up to $1,500 in creator matching
- $200 in ad credit

These reduce the platform cut to 0% for the first month and 1.8% for months 2–3, then snap back to 6%+1.8%. Plan your launch volume and post-promotion pricing accordingly.

## FBT (Fulfilled by TikTok) vs seller-shipped

| | FBT | Seller-shipped |
|---|---|---|
| Fulfillment fee | $2.50–$4.50/unit (size-based) | Variable |
| Shipping subsidy | None required | Seller absorbs or passes through |
| Return processing | $1.50/unit flat | $3.00+ per return |
| Storage | First 100 units free, then $0.40/cu ft/mo | None |
| 2-day shipping badge | Yes | No |

FBT improves your algorithm ranking but adds $2.50–$4.50 to fulfillment cost. For products under $15, the unit economics break with FBT.

## What changes the math most

| Lever | Impact per $30 product |
|---|---|
| Affiliate rate from 10% to 20% | −$3.90/unit |
| Return rate from 5% to 10% | −$0.40/unit |
| Switching from FBT to seller-shipped | saves $3.50, loses 2-day badge |
| Running shop ads at 10% ACoS | −$3.00/unit |
| Higher creator conversion (>4%) | +5–10% net |

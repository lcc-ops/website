A solo publisher puts 350 AI-assisted ebooks on Amazon KDP, clearing roughly 18,000 EUR over six months. Numbers self-reported. Below is what the math actually says once you stop reading it as a book business and start reading it as a library business.

## What the case claims

| Quantity | Value |
|---|---|
| Total books | 350 |
| Time window | 6 months |
| Total revenue | about 18,000 EUR (about 14,000 USD) |
| Per-book average | about 51 EUR |
| Per-book production cost | 4 - 8 EUR |
| Output cadence | 3 - 5 books / week |
| Editing time per book | 1 - 2 hours |
| Monthly income (after 3 months) | 1,000 - 3,000 USD |

All figures are self-reported. No invoices, no dashboard screenshots, no audit. Treat as data points from one operator's claim, not market-wide evidence.

## The arithmetic that actually matters

Skip the average. The case is built around a 20/80 distribution: 20% of titles carry 80% of revenue and most titles earn single-digit EUR. One concrete comparison from the case: **7 sci-fi titles earned 84 EUR, 7 dark-romance titles earned 1,092 EUR**, with the same AI, the same workflow, the same time investment. The 13x delta is entirely category and search volume.

Same shape as YouTube channels, niche content sites, app stores, and indie game catalogs. The unit economics work because the cost of a failure is small enough to absorb large numbers of them.

```
Net per book   = EUR (revenue - cost) about 51 - 6 = 45 EUR
Library net    = 45 x 350 about 15,750 EUR
That is the average. The 20/80 shape means:
Top 70 books (~20%)  about 14,400 EUR (80%)
Bottom 280 books      about  3,600 EUR (20%)
Per-book, the bottom books are losing money on editorial time.
```

The strategy only works at the library level, not the book level. **One book is a coin flip; 350 books is a portfolio.**

## The cost-structure rewrite, in concrete numbers

```
Traditional ghostwritten book:
  Cost:   EUR 300 - 800 per title
  Risk:   if it does not sell, you lose the entire cost
  Margin: need 50-100 copies sold before break-even

AI-assisted KDP book:
  Cost:   EUR 4 - 8 per title
  Risk:   single-digit EUR if it does not sell
  Margin: most titles recover cost in the first 2-4 weeks
```

The 50-100x reduction in per-title cost is the entire reason this model exists. With traditional cost, 350 unsold books = EUR 100K+ in losses; with AI cost, 350 unsold books = EUR 1,750 in losses. The risk budget for the library is bounded by appetite for time, not capital.

## Failure modes (per the case)

| Failure mode | Why it kills the math |
|---|---|
| Publishes one book, waits for revenue | Single book variance is too high to make decisions from. |
| Picks categories without search-volume data | The 13x delta between sci-fi and dark-romance is one example. Most categories return less than 50 EUR total across 7 books. |
| Ships unedited AI output | Negative reviews pile up, KDP account risk increases, organic ranking drops. |
| Skips cover, blurb, keyword research | Amazon discovery is search-driven. A poorly-packaged book is functionally invisible. |

The first three are about avoiding Amazon-side consequences. The fourth is about being seen at all.

## What the case does not cover

- **Refund and dispute rate.** A 1-2% refund rate on a 4 EUR cost is fine. A 10-15% rate on a poorly-edited title compounds into account-level risk.
- **Tax and platform fees.** KDP takes 30% on the 0.99-2.99 USD band; 65% on higher prices. Not visible in the case.
- **Ad spend.** Organic KDP income alone is slow. Many operators burn USD 200-500 on AMS ads per book to push visibility. The case says nothing about ad cost.
- **Account risk profile.** Multiple KDP accounts, IP-shared computers, sudden output volume triggers. Amazon's review team does review high-volume publishing bursts. The case does not address how to stay below the signal thresholds.

## Take-away

2-3 hours a day and minimal capital, and you can read a category's search-volume chart: this is a real, internally-consistent case. Skip the "AI wrote a book" framing entirely; the operation is a portfolio business running on a per-title cost of 4 EUR. **Category choice dominates; quality is table stakes; cover and keywords are the distribution mechanism.**

Do not copy the post's "publish 100 books in 3 months" cadence without an editor. The reason this works is not AI writing speed; it is that the cost-per-failure stayed small enough that the failures do not kill the library.

The arithmetic works because the cost-per-article fell 500x (from 5-10 USD per ghostwritten article to 0.013 USD per AI-assisted article). The model is keyword-volume compounding; the AI is the production cost floor.'
faq:
  - q: 'How much revenue does the case describe?'
    a: 'About 2,300 USD/month AdSense by month 6 across 3 sites. Cumulative 9,000 articles over the 6-month window, ~8,000 daily UV across the portfolio, ~360k monthly PV. Month 3 was around 170 USD. RPM (revenue per thousand pageviews) was 6-9 USD, normal for information-niche AdSense.'
  - q: 'What does it cost to run?'
    a: 'Domain registration: around 30 USD/year for 3 domains. Shared hosting: 12 USD/month. Claude Pro subscription: 20 USD/month. Total cash cost under 40 USD/month. The cost of the operator’s time is separate and not in the post.'
  - q: 'How does the keyword strategy work?'
    a: 'The operator only targets keywords with KD (keyword difficulty) below 15 and monthly search volume between 100 and 500. Big sites pass on these because the volume is too small to matter; small sites lose to AI volume. The 100-500 SV band is the gap where AI-powered volume production wins.'
  - q: 'What is the AI leverage point concretely?'
    a: 'Compare per-article cost: traditional ghostwritten content 5-10 USD per article, AI-assisted under 0.013 USD per article (6 months of Claude subscription spread across 9,000 articles = around 0.013 USD each). Same 9,000 articles cost 45,000-90,000 USD ghostwritten vs about 120 USD in subscription fees. That is a 500x compression.'
  - q: 'What does the operator actually do each day?'
    a: 'Around 1 hour per day, per the post: keyword selection, draft review and editing on the title and opening paragraph, automation publishing through WordPress or similar. The 15-20 articles per site per day are mostly AI-generated first drafts with minimal human pass-through.'
  - q: 'What can break the model?'
    a: 'Three risks the post calls out: (1) Google can change its AI content policy and de-rank the portfolio; (2) RPM is seasonal — Q1 is typically 60% of Q4 because advertisers spend less; (3) any single site being deindexed wipes out 1/3 of revenue. The 3-site, multi-niche, multi-domain strategy is a hedge against any single-point failure.'

A case profiles Hightouch, the AI marketing platform that landed Domino's, Spotify, and DoorDash as enterprise customers. The mechanism, as stated by Hightouch's co-founder Kashish Gupta, is a hard constraint: Domino's never lets AI generate a pizza. The pizza in every ad is real photography. AI generates only backgrounds, supporting elements, and contextual copy. The product is not the model. The product is the brand-safety constraint. The case landed ~$70M of net-new ARR in 20 months and pushed the company past $100M ARR. The structural claim is not about generation quality — it is about what is scarce in 2026 AI marketing. The model is commodity. The constraint is the moat. Below is the unit-economics logic, the category framing, and four gaps the case does not address.

## The mechanism

```
Brand-team request:
  "Generate a pizza ad for a new product launch"
   ↓
Hightouch constraint engine:
  - pizza image:     real photography (forbidden from AI generation)
  - background:      AI-generated, brand-color compliant
  - supporting elements: AI-generated, brand-safety compliant
  - copy:            AI-generated, legal-approved vocabulary
   ↓
Output:
  - pizza photo:     from Domino's brand asset library
  - background:      AI-generated
  - elements:        AI-generated
  - copy:            AI-generated
   ↓
Brand review:        human sign-off
   ↓
Final ad:            ships
```

The constraint is the product. Most brand teams have already tried opening a model, typing 'generate a pizza ad', and got burned. The output looks generic, off-brand, and is legally risky. The brand team needs a vendor that ships the constraint by default. Hightouch ships the constraint.

## Why the constraint is the moat

The structural claim is what is scarce in 2026 AI marketing. Generation is not scarce. Every brand team has API access to the same model providers. Brand-correctness is scarce. A model that knows Domino's brand colors, logo placement rules, legal vocabulary, and historical ad style is scarce. A vendor that ships this by default — and indemnifies the brand against legal risk on AI output — is scarce.

The commodity component:

```
Model API:        available to all
Prompting layer:  available to all
Brand-asset DB:   hard to build, hard to license
Legal-vocab DB:   hard to build, hard to maintain
Indemnification:  hard to underwrite
```

The first two are commodity. The last three are moats. Hightouch's product is the last three.

## Unit economics

```
Per-output operator cost:     $0.50–$5 (model + review)
Per-customer price (typical): $20K–$200K/year
Brand-marketing customer count: ~100–300 (implied from $70M net-new ARR)
Implied ARPU:                $50K–$300K/year
Customer concentration:      high (Domino's-class contracts dominate)
Net-new ARR per month:       ~$3.5M/month over 20 months
Total ARR:                   >$100M
```

The customer concentration is the unstated variable. $70M of net-new ARR likely sits on a small number of Fortune-500 brand contracts. Churn of one is catastrophic. The case does not address concentration directly.

## The category framing

The case lands on a category frame that matters: the product is not 'AI marketing'. The product is 'AI marketing with brand-safety guarantees by default'. The two categories have very different competitive structures:

```
"AI marketing":
  - competitors: Adobe, Salesforce, Shopify, Canva, OpenAI
  - pricing:      bundled, free, or low
  - moat:         none

"AI marketing with brand-safety guarantees":
  - competitors: Hightouch, Typeface, a few others
  - pricing:      $20K–$200K/year per brand
  - moat:         brand-asset DB, legal-vocab DB, indemnification
```

The first category is oversaturated. The second is not. The frame — 'constrained generation' rather than 'generation' — is the move.

## What the case does not cover

Four gaps.

1. **Sales cycle.** The case does not give average days from first call to signed contract. Brand-team enterprise sales typically run 6–18 months. A 20-month $70M ARR curve implies sales cycles that started 6–18 months before the ARR landed.
2. **Churn.** The case does not give a renewal rate. A 10%/year churn is the SaaS benchmark; brand-marketing churn can be higher when a CMO changes or when a brand consolidates vendors.
3. **Constraint cost.** The case does not give the human-review cost per output. If the brand-safety layer requires human sign-off on every output, the per-output cost is higher than the customer expects, and the gross margin compresses.
4. **Model commoditization risk.** The case does not address what happens if Shopify / Adobe / Salesforce ship a comparable brand-safety layer as a free bundled feature in their existing marketing clouds. The risk is real; the case does not address it.

## Take-away

The case is not 'Hightouch's AI is better'. The case is: in 2026, the scarce resource in AI marketing is not generation — it is the brand-correctness constraint that the major model providers refuse to ship. Hightouch ships the constraint, indemnifies the brand, and sells the constraint at a $20K–$200K/year per-brand price. The model is commodity; the constraint is the moat.

For most operators building AI products in 2026, the bottom line is: stop competing on model quality. Find a buyer who needs a constraint, and ship the constraint. The model is a commodity input. The constraint is a $20K–$200K/year line item.

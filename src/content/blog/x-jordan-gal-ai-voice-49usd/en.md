An AI voice product targeting local small businesses that miss calls. 6-person team. 90 days to first version. 8 months to 1M USD ARR. Single 49 USD/month plan throughout the launch. Cold start via ~1000 cold emails per day for 2 months. SEO + paid ads split 50/50 on acquisition. The interesting part is not 'AI makes voice calls' — it is the single-plan discipline and the cold-email CAC math. The funnel math, the single-plan discipline, and the four gaps the case does not cover, below.

## The unit economics at 1M USD ARR

```
At 1M USD ARR / 12 / 49 USD/month ≈ 1700 paying customers

Per customer per month:
  Revenue:                49 USD
  Voice infra (Twilio + model): 5–15 USD
  Net contribution:       34–44 USD

Annualized:
  Contribution per customer:    408–528 USD
  × 1700 customers:             693K–898K USD

Team cost (6 people, loaded):  1.2M USD / year
```

At 1M USD ARR, contribution margin (693K–898K USD) does not yet cover team cost (1.2M USD). The product is pre-profitable on team alone. To clear team cost, the operator needs roughly 2300–3000 paying customers, which the case implies is the next milestone.

## The cold-email CAC math

```
1000 emails/day × 60 days = 60K emails
Reply rate (SMB voice):          1–3%
Replies:                          600–1800
Demo-to-close (high intent):      10–30%
Closed customers:                 60–540

Per customer LTV:                 49 USD × 12 × 24-month avg = 1176 USD
CAC per closed customer:          5–50 USD
LTV/CAC ratio:                    24–235×

The economics work as long as cold-email reply rates hold.
```

The cold-email CAC math is the load-bearing assumption. At 1–3% reply rates in a high-intent voice vertical, the math works dramatically. If reply rates drop to 0.3–0.5% (which is closer to cold-email averages in 2026), the cohort economics compress.

## Why single-plan discipline

Three reasons push single-plan:

1. **Decision cost.** A small-business owner comparing 49 USD / 99 USD / 199 USD plans spends 10x more time deciding and often picks nothing. A single 49 USD plan removes decision friction.
2. **Support cost.** Multiple plans mean multiple setup paths, multiple feature gates, more support tickets. Single-plan means one setup flow, one feature set, fewer tickets.
3. **Up-sell path.** Once the customer is on 49 USD for 3–6 months, they ask 'I have 3 stores, can I get a discount?' That is the natural up-sell moment — driven by customer need, not by plan tier.

## What this case does not cover

- **Monthly churn rate.** At 49 USD/month with SMB churn patterns, 5–10% monthly churn is plausible. The case presents ARR, not net-retained ARR. A 7% monthly churn on 1700 customers means losing 119 per month and acquiring 119 to stay flat.
- **AI-disclosure compliance cost.** The case does not address how the product handles FCC and state-level AI-voice disclosure rules. A 2026 enforcement wave on AI-voice disclosure could require per-call announcement, increasing latency and reducing conversion.
- **Cold-email deliverability.** 1000/day for 2 months is 60K emails without addressing spam-filter rates or domain-reputation cost. Inbox providers throttle at high volumes; the case does not say how many sending domains are rotated.
- **Up-sell mechanics.** The case does not say how a 49 USD single-plan customer becomes a 99 USD or 199 USD customer. The implied mechanism is multi-location volume pricing, but the math is not shown.

## Bottom line

The model is real: 1M USD ARR in 8 months with 6 people and a single 49 USD plan. Cold-email CAC math is the load-bearing assumption — it works at 1–3% reply rates but compresses at 2026 averages of 0.3–0.5%. A reader replicating this should test cold-email reply rates in their vertical before committing 6 people for 90 days. The single-plan discipline is the core move that holds contribution margin and support cost down. At 1M USD ARR the product is not yet profitable on team alone — the next 1300 customers are the profitability milestone.

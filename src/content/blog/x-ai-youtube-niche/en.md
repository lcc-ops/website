faq:
  - q: 'What is a realistic YouTube CPM range in 2026?'
    a: 'For US audiences: finance, insurance, B2B SaaS, legal, and healthcare-adjacent content runs 18-45 USD per 1000 monetized views. Consumer entertainment, gaming, and most lifestyle content sits at 0.6-3 USD. The 30x spread is the entire game.'
  - q: 'How long does it take to reach 1000 subscribers on a fresh channel?'
    a: 'Median across monetizable niches is 4-9 months with weekly publishing and a clear niche. Channels that publish 3x/week in a high-CPM niche can hit 1K in 60-90 days, but they pay for it with a much higher per-video production cost.'
  - q: 'What does 10K subscribers actually pay per month?'
    a: 'At 10K subs in a finance / SaaS niche with US viewership, after clearing YouTube partner-program thresholds, creators commonly report 1.5-4K USD/month from AdSense alone. Sponsorships, affiliate, and product revenue stack on top.'
  - q: 'Is AI-generated content allowed by YouTube?'
    a: 'Yes, with disclosure requirements that vary by region. The bigger constraint is monetization: YouTube has demonetized channels that mass-produce templated AI content without any original framing. One creator voice still matters.'
  - q: 'What is the biggest mistake new AI-YouTube creators make?'
    a: 'Picking a niche for personal interest instead of CPM math. The creator who picks finance because they are good at finance, and never compares it to gaming, builds for two years on a 2 USD CPM. The math is the math.'
  - q: 'Can you start on YouTube with zero budget?'
    a: 'Yes, if you accept that the constraint becomes time, not money. A free screen recorder, free AI voice (ElevenLabs free tier, edge TTS), and a free editor (CapCut, DaVinci Resolve) clear the budget gate. The trade is 8-15 hours/week of production time per video.'

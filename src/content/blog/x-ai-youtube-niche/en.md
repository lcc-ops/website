The post in question is a prompt template: "I'm [age], interested in [topic], want to use AI to make money on YouTube, give me the top 5 CPM niches that fit me." The interesting part is not the template. The interesting part is what the answer has to look like for the math to work.

CPM (cost per mille — what advertisers pay per 1000 monetized views) varies by roughly 30x across monetizable YouTube niches. Picking the wrong niche on day one means spending the rest of the year on a 2-USD-per-1000-views base.

## The 30x CPM spread

A clean snapshot of US-audience CPM by niche as of mid-2026:

| Niche | CPM (USD/1000 views) | Why advertisers pay |
|---|---|---|
| Personal finance, investing, insurance | 28-45 | High LTV customer; regulated buyer pools |
| B2B SaaS, martech, dev tools | 22-38 | SMB and enterprise buyers consume video before purchase |
| Legal, tax, compliance | 18-32 | High-value professional services |
| Health-adjacent (insurance, pharmacy, equipment) | 15-28 | Patient-acquisition cost is high; regulated buyer pools |
| Career, technical recruiting | 12-22 | Recruiter ad budgets are large and per-hire ROI is trackable |
| Software tutorials, dev content | 10-18 | Developer tools compete heavily for awareness |
| Consumer tech reviews | 5-12 | High view volume; lower per-view value |
| Education, K-12 | 4-9 | School budget advertising is fragmented |
| Lifestyle, vlog, beauty, fashion | 1-4 | High view volume; broad, low-LTV audiences |
| Gaming | 0.6-2 | Massive view volume; gamer LTV is low and ad-block is high |
| Entertainment, meme, compilation | 0.4-1.5 | Same as gaming; ad-block rate exceeds 40% in many geos |

The spread between top and bottom is approximately 100x. The gap in earnings between a "sustainable creator business" and a "hobby that costs money" runs roughly 20x.

## What the subscriber bands actually pay

Working backwards from the niche table above, a US-skewed channel publishing 2 videos/week in a finance / SaaS / legal niche can expect roughly:

- **1K subs, ~30K monthly views.** AdSense: 540-1350 USD/month. Not a salary.
- **10K subs, ~250K monthly views.** AdSense: 4.5K-11K USD/month. One full-time income in low-cost geographies; a side income in higher-cost ones.
- **100K subs, ~2M monthly views.** AdSense: 36K-90K USD/month. Real business; sponsorships and product revenue typically double this.

The same channel in a gaming or entertainment niche at 100K subs clears 1.2K-4K USD/month from AdSense. The gap is the niche, not the work.

## Why AI changes the production economics

The post assumes AI is a content generator. The actual shift is that AI collapses the per-video production cost at the bottleneck steps — script drafting, voiceover, and rough cut editing. At pre-AI cost, a polished 12-minute video ran 200-600 USD in creator time alone. At AI-assisted cost, the same video takes 8-15 hours of creator time but no per-minute cash outlay.

That shift does not change CPM. It changes the break-even view count. The creator who produces 3 videos/week at near-zero marginal cost on a 25 USD CPM niche needs roughly 1,500 monetized views per video to clear the production-time opportunity cost. The creator on a 2 USD CPM niche needs roughly 19,000 views per video for the same break-even.

## What the post does not cover

- **Niche CPM drift.** CPM is not static. Finance and insurance CPMs rose with rate cycles; entertainment CPMs fell as ad-block adoption grew. The table is mid-2026; it will look different in 2027.
- **Geographic split.** US-skewed audiences are the only CPM tier that pays creator-salary numbers. India, SEA, LATAM audiences at the same sub count return 5-15% of US revenue.
- **Algorithm and "original voice" risk.** Channels that mass-produce templated AI content without an original framing get demonetized at scale. YouTube policy is enforced in waves; the next enforcement wave is the actual risk.
- **Sponsor revenue overlay.** AdSense is one revenue line. Channels in high-CPM niches typically layer 2-5x more revenue from sponsorships, affiliate, and product. The bands above are AdSense-only.
- **Burnout and production sustainability.** The math says 2-3 videos/week. The creator-side constraint is whether the creator can sustain that cadence for 12-24 months. Most who try this pace do not.
- **Multi-platform assumption.** YouTube is one platform. The same content on TikTok pays differently (creator fund, lower per-view), on Instagram Reels pays differently again, and on a newsletter pays differently again. The post assumes YouTube-only.

## Take-away

The CPM spread is the entire game. AI changes what it costs to make a video; it does not change what advertisers pay to reach a viewer. The creator who picks the niche on day one for CPM math, not personal interest, builds a viable business in 12-18 months. The creator who picks the niche for interest builds a hobby that costs money over the same time window. The prompt template in the post is the right shape — the answer it returns is what determines the outcome.

faq:
  - q: 'Which country has the highest mobile wallet penetration?'
    a: 'China — Alipay and WeChat Pay cover ~90% of urban consumers and ~70% of small merchants. South Korea follows with Kakao Pay and Samsung Pay; Japan with PayPay.'
  - q: 'What is the most-used payment method in Latin America?'
    a: 'Cards (Visa, Mastercard) for online, but offline cash voucher systems like Mexico''s OXXO and Brazil''s Boleto Bancário serve 30–40% of unbanked consumers. Mercado Pago is the fastest-growing wallet.'
  - q: 'Which European countries support instant bank transfers?'
    a: 'Sweden (Swish), Denmark (MobilePay), Norway (Vipps), Netherlands (iDEAL), Germany (Giropay/Sofort), Poland (Przelewy24), and the UK (Faster Payments). Instant is the default in Nordics.'
  - q: 'How does M-PESA work in Kenya?'
    a: 'M-PESA is a mobile phone-based stored value account run by Safaricom. Users deposit cash at agent shops, get an e-money balance, transfer via SMS, and withdraw at any agent. ~70% of Kenya''s adult population uses it.'
  - q: 'What is the difference between a wallet and a payment gateway?'
    a: 'A wallet (Alipay, PayPal, Apple Pay) holds stored value or links to a funding source and is presented at checkout. A gateway (Stripe, Adyen, PayU) is the technical bridge that processes a transaction between a merchant and the card network or bank.'
  - q: 'Which cross-border payment service is cheapest for small sellers?'
    a: 'For under $10k/month: Wise or Payoneer for payouts, PayPal for customer-facing. For over $50k/month: Airwallex or PingPong for FX hedging and lower per-transaction fees.'

Digital payments vary dramatically by region. East Asia runs on mobile wallets, Europe on cards and instant bank transfers, Latin America on a hybrid of cards and cash vouchers, Africa on mobile money. Here is the country-by-country map.

## Asia: the most diverse payment landscape

### East Asia

**China** has the world's highest mobile wallet penetration. Alipay and WeChat Pay cover ~90% of urban consumers and dominate in-person QR-code payments. Supporting wallets: JDB Pay, Best Pay, Baidu Wallet, UnionPay Wallet. Internet payment platforms: Alipay Online, TenPay, UnionPay Online, 99Bill, ChinaPnr. Mobile apps from every major bank and payment institution enable QR scanning and remote transfers.

Card networks: UnionPay (dominant domestically), Visa, Mastercard, JCB. Prepaid: carrier cards from China Unicom/Telecom/Mobile, plus retail gift cards. Cross-border: Alipay International, WeChat Pay International, UnionPay International, PingPong, Airwallex, LianLian Pay.

**Japan** combines mature card networks with transit-flavored prepaid cards. Wallets: PayPay, LINE Pay, Rakuten Pay, au WALLET. Internet payment: JCB Net Payment, Rakuten Online Payment, GMO Payment Gateway. Cards: JCB, Visa, Mastercard, Amex, plus domestic UFJ NICOS. Transit cards — Suica, Pasmo, ICOCA — extend into retail and vending machines.

**South Korea** has high concentration. Three wallets dominate: Kakao Pay, Samsung Pay, Toss. Internet payment: KCP, LG U+ Pay, SK Pay. Cards: BC Card, Shinhan Card, KB Card + international networks. Transit: T-Money, Cashbee. Cross-border: Kakao Pay International, NHN International.

**Mongolia** is bank-wallet-led: Mongol Bank mobile payment, Golomt Bank wallet, XacBank wallet.

### Southeast Asia

**Vietnam**: MoMo, ZaloPay, VNPay lead wallets. Internet: VNPay, OnePay, NganLuong. Cards: Vietcombank, VietinBank, AGRIBANK. Cross-border: VNPay International, MoMo International.

**Thailand**: TrueMoney, PromptPay, Rabbit LINE Pay, KBank wallet. PromptPay is a national instant payment system. Cards: Kasikornbank, Bangkok Bank, TMB Bank. Cross-border: TrueMoney International, Ascend Money International.

**Indonesia**: GoPay, OVO, Dana, ShopeePay. Gateways: Doku, Midtrans, Xendit. Cards: BCA, Mandiri, CIMB Niaga. Cross-border: Midtrans International, Xendit International.

**Singapore** (mature, financial-center-grade): GrabPay, Singtel Dash, DBS PayLah!, OCBC Pay Anyone, eNETS. Cards: DBS/POSB, OCBC, UOB + international networks. Cross-border: NETS International, DBS International.

**Malaysia**: Touch'n Go eWallet (transit-tied), GrabPay, Boost. Cards: Maybank, Public Bank, RHB. Gateway: iPay88, MOLPay. Transit prepaid: Touch'n Go card.

**Philippines**: GCash leads, followed by PayMaya, Dragonpay. Online bills: Bayad Center Online, ECPay. Cards: BPI, Security Bank, Metrobank. Cross-border: ECPay International, Dragonpay International.

### Central and West Asia

**Kazakhstan**: Halyk Bank wallet, National Bank e-wallet, Halyk Pay, Qiwi Wallet.
**Uzbekistan**: Uzcard as the domestic backbone.
**Kyrgyzstan**: Kassa24 wallet, PayNet KG.
**Turkey**: Garanti wallet, iyzico, PayU.
**Saudi Arabia**: STCPay, SADAD, mada mobile.
**Iran**: Shetab domestic network.
**Israel**: PayPal-led for online.
**UAE / Bahrain**: DubaiPay as regional hub.

## Europe: regulated, mature, instant

### Northern Europe

The Nordics are the global instant-payment leaders. Bank-backed wallets dominate personal transfers and retail:

- **Sweden**: Swish (instant), Klarna (BNPL), Trustly (bank-direct)
- **Denmark**: MobilePay, Dankort Online
- **Norway**: Vipps, BankAxept Internet

Cards: Visa, Mastercard, Maestro. Cross-border: PayPal, Wise, Klarna.

### Central and Eastern Europe

**Russia**: Qiwi Wallet, Yandex.Money. Gateways: Robokassa, 2Checkout.
**Poland**: Paysafecard, Dotpay, Przelewy24, PayU.
**Czech Republic**: GoPay.
**Ukraine**: Privat24.
**Germany**: Giropay, Sofortbanking (PayNow).
**Austria**: EPS.

### Western and Southern Europe

**UK / France**: Skrill, PayPal, Lemon Wallet, Paylib. Enterprise gateways: Stripe, Worldpay, Payzen. Mobile: Apple Pay and Google Pay dominate contactless. Cards: Visa, Mastercard, Maestro; Amex in premium segments.

**Italy**: PagoPA, PostePay.
**Spain**: BBVA Wallet, CaixaBank Wallet, Redsys.
**Portugal**: Multibanco.
**Greece**: Alpha e-Banking, Piraeus Online, VivoPay, Mpay.

## Africa, Oceania, Americas

### Africa: leapfrog into mobile

Mobile wallets are the backbone of African payments because traditional banking penetration is low:

- **Kenya, Tanzania**: M-PESA (Safaricom) — ~70% adult coverage in Kenya
- **Nigeria**: Paystack, Flutterwave, OPay, PalmPay
- **Ghana**: MTN Mobile Money, AirtelTigo Money
- **South Africa**: SnapScan, Zapper
- **Pan-African**: Chipper Cash, Vodacom M-Pesa

Internet payment gateways: Paystack, Flutterwave, Interswitch. Cross-border: Earthport, Adyen + domestic cross-border wallets.

### Oceania

**Australia**: humm (BNPL), PayPal, Apple Pay, Google Pay. Gateway: eWAY. Vodafone Wallet for telco.

### North America: the global benchmark

**Wallets**: Apple Pay, Google Pay, Samsung Pay (contactless); Venmo, Cash App (P2P); PayPal, Amazon Wallet, Walmart Pay (retail).

**Gateways**: PayPal, Stripe, 2Checkout, Worldpay, Braintree, Authorize.Net — these power a large share of global ecommerce.

**Cards**: Visa, Mastercard, Amex, Discover, JCB, Diners Club — six networks cover virtually every scenario.

**Prepaid**: iTunes, Starbucks, MetroCard.

**Cross-border**: PayPal, Wise, Western Union, Xoom, OFX.

**POS**: Verifone, Ingenico, First Data dominate physical retail terminals.

### Latin America: digital transition with cash roots

**Wallets**: Mercado Pago (largest, tied to Mercado Libre), RappiPay (delivery-tied), PicPay (Brazil), Clip (Mexico), Yape (Peru), DaviPlata (Colombia).

**Gateways**: PayPal, Mercado Pago Online, SafetyPay, PagSeguro, PayU Latam, 2Checkout.

**Cards**: Visa, Mastercard + domestic (Elo, Hipercard in Brazil; Banamex in Mexico; Banco do Brasil, Itaú).

**The cash voucher layer** is unique to LatAm:

- **Mexico**: OXXO convenience store cash vouchers — covers ~30% of online payments
- **Brazil**: Boleto Bancário — bank/post office cash slips, valid 1–3 days
- **Argentina**: Pago Fácil — third-party bill payment agents

These serve the 30–40% of consumers without bank accounts or cards.

## What this means for cross-border sellers

1. **Don't assume global checkout = global cards.** M-PESA, OXXO, Boleto, iDEAL, Alipay each capture meaningful local volume that cards don't reach.
2. **Local payment methods convert 20–50% better** than card-only checkouts in their home markets.
3. **The cheapest cross-border payout** depends on volume: Wise/Payoneer under $10k/month, Airwallex/PingPong above $50k/month.
4. **Regulatory divergence matters** — PSD2 in Europe, PBoC licensing in China, RBI in India each impose different rules on foreign PSPs.

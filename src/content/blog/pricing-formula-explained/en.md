Most sellers price by gut feel: *cost × 3 sounds right*. That works until the third month when the books show a loss. Here's the formula professional cross-border sellers actually use.

## The formula

```
price = (cost + shipping + fixed fees) ÷ (1 − commission − payment − target margin)
```

Where:

- **cost** = supplier price in USD
- **shipping** = per-order shipping cost to customer
- **fixed fees** = per-order platform fees (Shopify $0.30, PayPal $0.49 in some markets)
- **commission + payment** = combined percentage fee
- **target margin** = net profit % you want to keep

## Worked example 1 — branded Shopify product

- Cost: $8
- Shipping: $3.50
- Shopify Payments fee: 2.9% + $0.30
- Target margin: 35%

```
price = (8 + 3.5 + 0.3) ÷ (1 − 0.029 − 0.35)
price = 11.8 ÷ 0.621
price = $19.00
```

Profit per unit: $19.00 × (1 − 0.029) − 11.80 = **$6.65**

## Worked example 2 — Etsy handmade

- Cost: $12 (materials + labor)
- Shipping: $5 (with $1 packaging)
- Etsy transaction fee: 6.5%
- Etsy payment processing: 3% + $0.25
- Target margin: 40%

```
price = (12 + 5 + 0.25) ÷ (1 − 0.065 − 0.03 − 0.40)
price = 17.25 ÷ 0.505
price = $34.16
```

## Worked example 3 — Amazon FBA

- Cost: $6
- Amazon referral fee: 15%
- FBA fulfillment: $4.50
- Target margin: 25%

```
price = (6 + 4.5) ÷ (1 − 0.15 − 0.25)
price = 10.5 ÷ 0.60
price = $17.50
```

The break-even price (0% margin) is $13.13. Anything above that is profit, anything below is a loss.

## Why markup-based pricing fails

A 3× markup on $8 gives $24. But:

| Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| Revenue | $24.00 |
| Shopify fee (2.9% + $0.30) | −$1.00 |
| Payment processing (already included above) | — |
| Shipping | −$3.50 |
| Cost | −$8.00 |
| **Net profit** | **$11.50** |

That's a 48% net margin, not the 35% you wanted. The 3× rule accidentally over-prices low-cost items and under-prices high-cost ones. The formula scales correctly across the entire catalog.

## Adjusting for returns

Add 5–15% to your cost line depending on category:

| Category | Return rate buffer |
|---|---|
| Apparel | 15% |
| Electronics | 8% |
| Home goods | 6% |
| Beauty | 4% |
| Books/media | 3% |

For 15% returns on the first example, raise cost from $8 to $9.20:

```
price = (9.20 + 3.5 + 0.3) ÷ (1 − 0.029 − 0.35)
price = $19.95
```

## Adjusting for advertising

If you run paid traffic, your true margin target is lower. A 35% margin with 20% ad cost of sales (ACoS) means you're keeping 15% net. Either raise prices or reduce ACoS.

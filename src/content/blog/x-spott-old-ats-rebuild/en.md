Spott — 3 founders from McKinsey / BCG / Bain, 2024 launch, 8x revenue growth in 6 months to 1M USD/month (12M USD annualized). YC W25, 3.2M USD seed (Base10). The product replaces Bullhorn, Loxo, Vincere — the 20-year-old ATS / CRM stack used by recruiting agencies. The interesting part is not 'AI rebuilds recruiting software' — it is that the boring old-software replacement is the highest-payoff AI play. Demand is validated, pain is acute, migration barrier locks the win. The unit economics, the migration moat, and the four gaps the case does not cover, below.

## Why boring old-software replacement is the best AI play

```
Old software replacement pattern:
  1. Demand is already validated
     (customer already pays for the old software)
  2. Customer pain is already acute
     (existing software hasn't been rebuilt in 10–20 years)
  3. Migration barrier locks the win
     (data migration is high-stakes, customer cannot easily switch back)

Spott's case:
  Old software:   Bullhorn / Loxo / Vincere (20 years old)
  Customer pain:  Recording a candidate takes 10+ clicks
  Migration:      4-week zero-downtime data migration
  Lock-in:        Once moved, switching back is career-ending for the recruiter
```

The 3 reasons compound. A reader scanning AI plays should weight boring old-software replacement over greenfield AI products because the demand side is solved. The risk is execution, not market.

## The unit economics at 1M USD/month

```
Recruiting agency cost structure:
  Senior recruiter loaded:        80K–150K USD/year
  ATS seat (Bullhorn, legacy):    100–300 USD/seat/month
  Admin time per recruiter:       30–40% of working hours

Spott pricing (anchored against recruiter loaded cost):
  Per recruiter pricing:          500–1500 USD/month
  Saves 50% of admin time:        40K–75K USD/year value per recruiter

At 1M USD/month run rate:
  Implied customer count:         50–200 mid-sized agencies
  Implied avg deal size:          5K–20K USD/month per agency
```

The pricing math jumps 10–50x compared to incumbent ATS seats. The reason is the anchor — Spott sells against recruiter loaded cost (100K+ USD/year), not against software seat cost (1.2K–3.6K USD/year). A 500 USD/month price tag against a 100K USD/year loaded recruiter looks like a no-brainer for the agency owner.

## Why the migration is the load-bearing product feature

The 4-week migration is what actually sells the product. The new UI is what closes the demo, but the migration is what closes the deal. Three reasons:

1. **Data gravity.** A recruiting agency's candidate database is its moat. 5 years of placements, 100K+ candidate profiles, custom notes, integration with job boards and LinkedIn Recruiter. Losing this in a botched migration is a career-ending event for the recruiter who pushed the switch.
2. **Switching cost.** Once the data is in Spott, with custom fields and integrations re-built, the cost of switching back to Bullhorn is enormous. The migration barrier locks in the win for 3+ years.
3. **Sales cycle acceleration.** A migration plan that fits on one slide accelerates the procurement cycle from 6 months to 4–6 weeks. The procurement office signs because the migration risk is bounded.

## What this case does not cover

- **Customer count and concentration.** 1M USD/month does not say whether 50 large customers or 200 mid-sized customers. A 50-customer book has 6% monthly churn risk if one customer leaves; a 200-customer book is more diversified.
- **Migration team capacity.** The 4-week migration is high-touch engineering. 10 parallel migrations need a 10-person migration team. The case does not show how the team scales with customer count.
- **Pricing per recruiter vs per agency.** The case does not say whether Spott bills per recruiter seat or per agency flat fee. Per-recruiter pricing expands with agency growth; flat-fee pricing has harder expansion.
- **Refund or churn rate.** Recruiting agencies churn when a placement slows or a recruiter leaves. Net-retained ARR is a different (lower) number than gross ARR.

## Bottom line

The model is real: 3 consulting refugees, 6 months to 1M USD/month, replacing 20-year-old recruiting software. The core move is pricing against recruiter loaded cost, not against software seat — the deal size jumps 10–50x. The migration is the load-bearing product feature; a 4-week zero-downtime migration locks the customer for 3+ years. A reader replicating this should pick 1 old-software vertical (recruiting, accounting, legal, customer-support), build the migration first, then the new UI. The boring old-software replacement is the highest-payoff AI play in 2026 because demand is validated and the migration barrier locks the win.

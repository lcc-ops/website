A case profiles an AI-generated short drama launched on overseas platform YourChannel. Title: 'Revenge of Persia'. The author is a Chinese operator. The audience is Persian / Middle East. The 72-hour revenue: $586,700, roughly 4M RMB. The production is AI-generated across script, storyboard, voice, and edit. The case lands on the unit economics of AI short-drama overseas in 2026: a single 70-episode vertical drama, 72 hours from launch, $586K revenue, near-zero production cost. Below is the unit-revenue curve, the breakeven math, and four gaps the case does not address.

## The unit economics comparison

```
Chinese short-drama platform:
  - ARPU per paid unlock:    $0.50–$3
  - 70-episode title revenue: $50K–$300K
  - Production cost (AI):    $500–$5,000
  - Lifetime:                30–60 days
  - Audience:                domestic, hundreds of millions

Overseas short-drama platform (YourChannel-class):
  - ARPU per paid unlock:    $5–$20
  - 70-episode title revenue: $200K–$2M
  - Production cost (AI):    $500–$5,000
  - Lifetime:                7–14 days (compressed)
  - Audience:                regional, 1–10M viewers
```

The structural argument is the ARPU gap, not the audience-size gap. The overseas audience is one-tenth the size of a Chinese hit. The per-viewer spend is 5–10x higher. The net revenue per title is 2–3x. The model is: smaller audience, much higher ARPU, faster revenue curve.

## The 72-hour revenue curve

```
Hour 0:    launch
Hour 0–6:  early paid unlocks from organic traffic
Hour 6–24: platform promotion if early signal is strong
Hour 24–48: peak paid unlocks, peak ARPU realization
Hour 48–72: tail of peak
Hour 72+:  long tail, 10–20% of remaining lifetime
```

The case states $586K in 72 hours. The lifetime is not given, but comparable AI short-drama titles in 2026 see 70–85% of lifetime revenue in the first 72 hours. The implied lifetime revenue is $700K–$850K, with 10–30% margin to the operator after platform share (typically 30–50%) and refund / chargeback.

## The breakeven math

```
Production cost:               $500–$5,000
Average ARPU per unlock:       $10 (assumed)
Breakeven paid unlocks:        50–500
72-hour paid unlocks:          ~58,600
Margin at 72 hours:            $580K+ (revenue - production)
Platform share (typical 30–50%): -$175K to -$293K
Refund / chargeback (5–15%):   -$30K to -$90K
Net to operator:               ~$200K–$375K
```

The case does not give the per-unlock ARPU, the platform share, or the refund rate. The implied net to operator is wide — $200K–$375K — but the order of magnitude is consistent with the public Chinese-operator AI-short-drama cases of 2026.

## Why the model works in 2026

Three reasons.

1. **AI compresses production cost to <1% of revenue.** A $500–$5,000 production cost against a $200K–$2M revenue title is 0.025–2.5% production ratio. A traditional (non-AI) short-drama production cost is 10–30% of revenue. The AI cost compression is the entire business model.
2. **The overseas ARPU gap is structural.** Persian / Middle East / Southeast Asia viewers pay 5–10x per unlock vs Chinese viewers. The platform can pay the operator 50–70% of that higher ARPU and still be profitable.
3. **The 72-hour curve matches the AI production cycle.** An operator can ship 1–3 titles per week. The 72-hour revenue curve clears the slot. The compound weekly output is the scale mechanism.

## What the case does not cover

Four gaps.

1. **Production cost.** The case states AI-generated but does not name the dollar figure. The implied range is $500–$5,000; the wide range matters for the breakeven math.
2. **Marketing cost.** Trending on YourChannel requires either paid placement or strong organic traction. The case does not address CAC. If the operator pays $50K for a trending slot, the net margin compresses significantly.
3. **Refund / chargeback rate.** Overseas short-drama platforms see 5–15% chargeback from buyers who regret the unlock. The case does not address this. At 10% chargeback, the operator loses ~$60K of the headline number.
4. **IP risk.** The title 'Revenge of Persia' borrows from a Persian historical narrative. The case does not address cultural-IP risk or platform-content-policy risk. A platform-side takedown in week 2 would zero out the long tail.

## Take-away

The case is not 'one AI short-drama made $586K'. The case is: AI short-drama overseas in 2026 has a structurally better ARPU curve than the Chinese equivalent, and the AI production cost collapses the breakeven to single-digit paid unlocks. The 72-hour window is the model — fast content, fast revenue, low tail. The weekly shipping cadence is the scale mechanism.

For most operators reading this case, the bottom line is: stop competing on Chinese short-drama platforms. Pick an overseas platform with a 5–10x ARPU gap. Use AI to collapse production cost to <1% of revenue. Ship 1–3 titles per week. The 72-hour curve compounds at the weekly cadence.

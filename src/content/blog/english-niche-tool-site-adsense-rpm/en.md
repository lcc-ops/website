A 27-year cross-border operator runs 10 English micro tool sites in finance, insurance, legal, and real-estate verticals. Each site is 10–30 pages, each page targets one long-tail query. AI produces the content in 2–3 hours per site. Total infra cost is under 120 USD/year for the matrix. Below is the math, the RPM shape, and four gaps the case does not cover.

## What the case describes

| Dimension | Reading |
|---|---|
| Site count | 10 |
| Pages per site | 10–30 |
| Per-page query | One long-tail (mortgage for [city], insurance for [state]) |
| Vertical | Finance / insurance / legal / real estate |
| Production time | 2–3 hours per site (AI) |
| Infra cost | < 120 USD/year for 10 sites |
| Stack | Cloudflare Pages + Hugo/Astro + AdSense |

The shape is "10 narrow sites at 10 long-tail queries each", not "one big site at one big query". The matrix diversifies ranking risk across 100 positions.

## Why RPM dominates the math

Google AdSense RPM in 2026 varies roughly 5x by vertical:

| Vertical | RPM (USD per 1000 PV) |
|---|---|
| Finance | 30–60 |
| Insurance | 28–55 |
| Legal | 26–52 |
| Real estate | 25–50 |
| Entertainment | 6–12 |
| Generic content | 4–10 |

Same 10,000 pageviews:
- Finance site: 300–600 USD
- Entertainment site: 60–120 USD

The vertical choice is the revenue multiplier before any traffic work happens. Picking "calculator for [city]" over "best movies 2026" is a 5x revenue shift.

## The unit economics

```
Per site:
  Domain:                    8–12 USD/year
  Cloudflare Pages:          free
  Hugo / Astro:              free
  SSL / CDN:                 free
  AI content production:     2–3 hours developer time
  Per-site annual cost:      under 12 USD/year

10-site matrix:
  Total infra:               under 120 USD/year
  Total content time:        20–30 hours once-off

Conservative traffic model:
  10 sites × 100 daily PV    = 1,000 PV/day = 30,000 PV/month
  At 35 USD RPM (finance mid-point):
  Monthly revenue:           1,050 USD

Aggressive traffic model (6 months in):
  10 sites × 300 daily PV    = 3,000 PV/day = 90,000 PV/month
  At 35 USD RPM:
  Monthly revenue:           3,150 USD
```

The cost basis is essentially zero — the operator can run the matrix at single-digit annual cost. The revenue is bounded by how many of the 100 positions rank, and how fast.

## Why the matrix shape matters

Long-tail queries have low individual search volume — 100–500 searches per month per query. They also have very low competition. A site ranking for "mortgage payment calculator for [city]" in a 200K-population city faces 2–3 weak competitors, not the multi-thousand competitor set of "mortgage calculator".

The matrix diversifies ranking risk across 100 positions. If 50 of 100 rank, the model works at the conservative line. If 80 rank, the model works at the aggressive line. The cost basis is the same either way.

This is the opposite of "one big site betting on one big keyword". The one-big-site model loses if the keyword does not rank. The matrix model loses only if many keywords do not rank.

## Production risks

| Risk | Why it bites |
|---|---|
| Google Helpful Content policy | Sites that look like AI slop with no user value get demoted. A pure-text calculator page with no working calculator is exactly the kind of content the policy targets. The site needs a real calculator, not a text description of one. |
| Source accuracy | Finance and insurance numbers must be real and current. A calculator showing wrong tax rates triggers user complaints and ranking drops. The AI prompt has to anchor against authoritative data sources (state insurance commissions, IRS publications, federal reserve rate feeds). |
| AdSense approval | High-RPM finance and insurance niches have stricter AdSense review. Sites get rejected at higher rates. The case does not say what fraction of the 10 sites passed review on first submission. |
| Update cadence | Long-tail queries like "car insurance comparison [state] 2026" age out. Without an annual update rhythm, rankings decay. The site needs a maintenance loop, not a one-shot build. |

## What the case does NOT cover

- **Per-site actual revenue.** The case estimates 1,050–3,150 USD/month across the matrix but does not show per-site dashboards. The 100-position ranking distribution is the unstated variable.
- **Realistic ranking timeline.** New domains take 3–6 months to rank on long-tail queries. The case implies this but does not show the curve. The first 3 months of a new matrix are typically near-zero revenue.
- **AdSense approval rate at the niche.** Finance and insurance sites face stricter review. Rejection rate is unstated. A 50% rejection rate on the matrix changes the model materially.
- **Liability posture.** If a user makes a financial decision based on a wrong calculator result, the site has no shield. The case does not address disclaimer wording or terms-of-use posture.

## Bottom line

The English micro tool site matrix is a real, internally consistent model. The cost basis is essentially zero. The revenue is bounded by ranking distribution, which is bounded by production quality. The right move is to ship a real calculator (not a text description of one), anchor against authoritative data sources, accept the 3–6 month wait, and treat the matrix as a 100-position portfolio, not a single bet.

The 5x RPM gap between finance and entertainment makes the vertical choice the largest lever. Picking "mortgage calculator for [city]" over "best movies 2026" is the difference between roughly 1,050 USD/month and 90 USD/month at the same traffic level.

For an indie operator with 40–60 hours of upfront time, this is a real model. The risk is not in the build — the risk is in the 3–6 month wait and the rank distribution across the 100 positions.

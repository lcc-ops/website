faq:
  - q: 'What does the case describe?'
    a: 'A 27-year cross-border operator runs 10 English micro tool sites. Each site has 10–30 pages, each page targets one long-tail query (mortgage payment calculator for [city], car insurance comparison [state] 2026, LLC formation cost [state]). Verticals are high-RPM niches: finance, insurance, legal, real estate. AI does all the content production in 2–3 hours per site. Stack is Cloudflare Pages (free), Hugo or Astro static site, domain 8–12 USD/year.'
  - q: 'Why does RPM dominate?'
    a: 'Google AdSense RPM (revenue per 1000 pageviews) varies 5x by vertical in 2026: finance 30–60 USD, insurance 28–55 USD, legal 26–52 USD, real estate 25–50 USD, entertainment 6–12 USD. Same 10,000 pageviews = 300–600 USD in finance versus 60–120 USD in entertainment. Picking the vertical sets the revenue multiplier before any traffic work happens.'
  - q: 'What is the unit economics?'
    a: 'Per site infra cost: domain 8–12 USD/year + Cloudflare Pages free + Hugo/Astro free = under 12 USD/year per site. 10 sites: under 120 USD/year. AI content cost: 2–3 hours per site at developer-time value (low, since the prompt is templated). Conservative traffic model: 10 sites × 100 daily pageviews = 1,000 pageviews/day = 30,000/month. At 35 USD finance RPM mid-point: 1,050 USD/month. Aggressive model at 6 months (some sites rank): 3,150 USD/month.'
  - q: 'Why does the matrix shape matter?'
    a: 'Because long-tail queries have low individual search volume (100–500 searches/month per query) but very low competition. 10 sites × 10 pages = 100 query positions. Each position ranks for one specific long-tail. The matrix diversifies across 100 queries instead of betting on one site. If 50 of 100 positions rank, the model works. If 80 rank, the model pays well.'
  - q: 'What are the production risks?'
    a: 'Four. (1) Google Helpful Content policy — sites that look like "AI slop" without user value get demoted. A pure-text calculator page with no working calculator is the kind of content the policy targets. (2) Source accuracy — finance and insurance numbers must be real and current; a calculator showing wrong tax rates triggers user complaints and ranking drops. (3) AdSense approval — high-RPM finance niches have stricter AdSense review; sites get rejected at higher rates. (4) Update cadence — long-tail queries like "2026" age out; the site needs an update rhythm or rankings decay.'
  - q: 'What does the case skip?'
    a: 'Four gaps. (1) Per-site actual revenue — the case estimates 1,000–3,150 USD/month but does not show per-site dashboards. (2) Realistic ranking timeline — new domains take 3–6 months to rank; the case implies this but does not show the curve. (3) AdSense approval rate at the niche — finance insurance sites face stricter review; rejection rate is unstated. (4) Refund / dispute surface — if a user loses money on a calculator result, the site has no liability shield; the case does not address disclaimer posture.'

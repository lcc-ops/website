ePacket was the default for Chinese sellers shipping small parcels to the US and EU. It is being phased out through 2026, replaced by a fragmented set of carriers with different price, speed and tracking characteristics. Here is what works now.

## What changed

USPS stopped accepting ePacket inbound in mid-2023. Most China Post lines migrated to Cainiao Standard, which keeps similar pricing but adds a tracking step. Sellers still listing ePacket on Etsy or Shopify risk buyer complaints when tracking gaps appear.

The good news: alternatives now cover the same price points. The bad news: choosing between them is more complex.

## Replacement matrix (0.5–2 kg to US)

| Carrier | Transit (days) | Cost per kg | Tracking | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **YunExpress Economy** | 12–20 | $4.80 | Semi-tracked | Cheapest tracked option, slower |
| **YunExpress Standard** | 10–15 | $6.20 | End-to-end | Stronger tracking, faster |
| **Cainiao Standard** | 14–25 | $3.80 | Partial | Cheapest but tracking gaps |
| **Cainiao Premium** | 8–12 | $7.20 | End-to-end | AliExpress default, fastest |
| **Yanwen Economic Air** | 18–28 | $3.50 | Weak | Cheapest, only for low-value items |
| **SF International** | 7–12 | $6.80 | End-to-end | Best speed, premium price |
| **Asendia (US-bound)** | 12–18 | $5.40 | End-to-end | USPS handoff, good for B2C |

For comparison, ePacket was $3.50–$5.00/kg, 7–14 days, end-to-end tracking. The replacements cost 10–20% more for tracked, or are cheaper with weaker tracking.

|---|---|---|---|
| **YunExpress EU** | 12–22 | $4.20 | End-to-end |
| **Cainiao Standard EU** | 16–28 | $3.20 | Partial |
| **DHL eCommerce** | 8–14 | $7.50 | End-to-end |
| **Asendia EU** | 14–20 | $5.10 | End-to-end |
| **GLS跨境** | 10–18 | $6.40 | End-to-end |

EU shipping is 5–10% more expensive per kg than US, with longer transit on the cheaper services. German customs adds 1–3 days randomly.

## Picking a carrier by product

**Cheap, low-value items (under $10):** Yanwen Economic or Cainiao Standard. Tracking gaps are acceptable for the price.

**Mid-value items ($10–$50):** YunExpress Standard or Asendia. Tracking needs to be presentable to buyers.

**High-value or time-sensitive:** SF International or Cainiao Premium. The $2–3/kg premium is worth the speed.

**Heavy items (over 2 kg):** Sea transit via YunExpress Sea or Asendia Sea. $1.80–$3.50/kg, 30–50 days.

## Working with freight forwarders

For sellers doing 200+ parcels/month, freight forwarders aggregate volume and pass through carrier rates. The two main paths:

1. **API-integrated platforms** (Easyship, ShipStation, ShipBob) — connect to your store, auto-pick cheapest carrier
2. **Direct carrier accounts** — YunExpress, Cainiao, SF International all offer direct shipper accounts

Direct accounts give 15–30% lower rates but require monthly volume (usually 500+ parcels).

## Tracking quality matters for Etsy more than Shopify

Etsy buyers look at tracking daily. A 3-day gap with no update is a "where is my order?" message. Shopify buyers are more patient.

If you sell on Etsy, prefer end-to-end tracking (YunExpress Standard, SF International, Asendia). If Shopify-only, semi-tracked services (YunExpress Economy, Cainiao Standard) are fine.

## The new "free shipping" math

Old ePacket pricing let sellers offer "free shipping" with $3.50–$5.00 baked into product cost. New carriers at $4.20–$7.20/kg change that math:

- A 0.5 kg product: shipping goes from $2.00 to $2.50–$3.60
- A 1.0 kg product: shipping goes from $4.00 to $4.80–$7.20
- A 2.0 kg product: shipping goes from $8.00 to $9.00–$14.40

Sellers with sub-$15 products need to either raise prices, accept thinner margins, or move to sea transit on slow-moving items.

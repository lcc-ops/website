faq:
  - q: 'What replaced ePacket for China to US shipping?'
    a: 'YunExpress, Cainiao Standard, Yanwen, SF International and Asendia are the main replacements. For tracked, semi-tracked and untracked tiers, expect 10–18 day transit and $3.50–$7.20/kg air freight.'
  - q: 'Is ePacket still available in 2026?'
    a: 'Largely no. USPS stopped accepting ePacket inbound in 2023, and most China Post lines have been migrated to Cainiao Standard. Sellers still using ePacket on old listings should expect service degradation or refund risk.'
  - q: 'What is the cheapest China to US shipping method?'
    a: 'For 0.5–2 kg parcels, Yanwen and YunExpress economy air run $3.50–$5.20/kg to the US. Sea transit is $1.80–$3.50/kg but takes 30–50 days.'
  - q: 'Which China to US carrier has the best tracking?'
    a: 'SF International and Asendia provide end-to-end tracking with US carrier handoff (USPS, FedEx). Cainiao Standard tracking is partial in some destinations. Yanwen tracking is weakest.'
  - q: 'How long does shipping from China to the US take now?'
    a: "10–18 days air for tracked services, 12–22 days semi-tracked, 30–50 days for sea/ePacket-equivalent economy. Compared to ePacket's 7–14 days, budget an extra 3–5 days for transit."

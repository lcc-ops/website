"Dropshipping is free to start" is the lie. Here's what a real store actually pays.

## The complete cost stack

A store selling a $50 product via AliExpress dropshipping:

| Cost | Amount | % of revenue |
|---|---|---|
| **Revenue** | **$50.00** | **100%** |
| Product cost | $12.00 | 24% |
| Shipping to customer | $4.50 | 9% |
| Shopify Basic | $1.30 (allocated) | 2.6% |
| Shopify Payments | $1.75 (2.9% + $0.30) | 3.5% |
| Apps (5 × $20/mo allocated) | $3.30 | 6.6% |
| Email tool | $0.50 | 1% |
| **Subtotal before ads** | **$26.65** | **53.3%** |
| **Gross margin** | **$23.35** | **46.7%** |
| Ad spend (Google + Meta) | $10.00 | 20% |
| **Net margin before returns** | **$13.35** | **26.7%** |
| Returns (8% of orders × $50 × cost basis 30%) | $1.20 | 2.4% |
| Chargebacks (1% × $15 fee) | $0.15 | 0.3% |
| Refund processing (refund fees kept by Stripe) | $0.20 | 0.4% |
| Customer service time (allocated) | $0.50 | 1% |
| **Real net margin** | **$11.30** | **22.6%** |

That 22.6% looks fine. Now add the things most beginners forget:

## Hidden costs that erode profit

### 1. Slow shipping refunds

US customers expect delivery in 5–7 days. AliExpress shipping takes 15–25 days. ~5% of customers request partial refunds ("where is my order?") or full refunds.

Average payout on a slow-shipping complaint: $10 per affected customer. On 100 orders/month, that's $50 in refunds for issues you can't prevent.

### 2. "Item not as described" claims

Once the customer receives a $12 AliExpress product, ~3% file "not as described" claims. PayPal and Stripe typically side with the buyer. Cost per claim: $20 product refund + $15 chargeback fee + $5 in time = **$40 per claim**.

### 3. Ad creative costs

Successful stores test 5–10 creatives per month. Each video creative costs $50–300 if outsourced, or 4–8 hours if done in-house. Most beginners don't budget for this.

### 4. Influencer / UGC costs

If you use influencers or pay for UGC, $100–500 per creator. Without tracking, ROI is unproven.

### 5. Subscription fatigue

Apps that you tried and stopped using still charge. Audit monthly.

### 6. Currency conversion loss

Stripe charges 1% on currency conversion. PayPal charges 3–4%. Receiving USD but paying suppliers in CNY is a 2–3% loss by default.

### 7. Inventory volatility

Dropshippers don't control inventory. A product can go out of stock mid-campaign. Wasted ad spend on out-of-stock items: 5–10% of total ad budget.

### 8. Customer service time

Each order generates ~0.1 hours of CS time. At $20/hour fully loaded, that's $2 per order. Most beginners underestimate this.

## The "winning product" trap

Gurus sell "winning product" lists. A typical "winning product" generates 30–60 days of strong sales, then fades as competitors copy it. By month 4, ROAS drops from 4× to 1.5× and the product is dead.

Realistic scenario:
- Month 1–2: $10k revenue, 25% net margin = $2,500 profit
- Month 3: $8k revenue, 20% margin = $1,600 profit
- Month 4+: $4k revenue, 10% margin = $400 profit
- New product needed

A store needs 3–5 active winning products at any time to maintain revenue.

## Why most dropshipping stores fail

1. **No repeat customers** — pure cold traffic. Each new sale costs the same as the last.
2. **Single product focus** — one winner fades, revenue collapses.
3. **Underestimated CAC** — beginners don't account for ad spend + creative costs.
4. **No brand** — commodity products get compared on price alone.
5. **Slow shipping** — AliExpress dropshipping loses to Amazon on speed.

## How to win at dropshipping in 2026

- **Use US/EU suppliers** — CJ Dropshipping, Zendrop, or local 3PLs. Pay 20% more, ship in 5 days, lose fewer customers.
- **Build a niche brand** — pick a category and own it. Generic stores compete on price; branded stores compete on identity.
- **Email/SMS list from day 1** — repeat customers are 3× more valuable than first-time.
- **Bundle products** — $50 average order value instead of $25. Same ad spend, double revenue.
- **Test 5 products/week** — most will fail; that's the game.
- **Track net profit, not revenue** — revenue is vanity; margin is sanity.

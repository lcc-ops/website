A case profiles an operator who, in roughly 9 months, made nearly $30,000 from AI-written articles on Medium + SEO + selling digital products. The mechanism: bulk AI article production (2 articles per day) + Medium distribution + SEO-optimized titles + a digital product funnel. The case is not 'AI content works'. The case is the unit economics: 2 articles/day for 9 months is ~540 articles, $30K revenue is $55/article average. The structural claim: this is a content-pipeline business, not an AI-magic business. Below is the per-article math, the digital-product backend, and four gaps the case does not address.

## The per-article unit economics

```
Per-article production cost:        $1–$5  (AI model + minor editing)
Per-article distribution cost:      $0     (Medium organic + SEO)
Per-article pageviews (lifetime):   200–1,000  (long-tail)
Per-article Medium partner revenue: $0.20–$5  ($0.10–$0.50 per 100 views)
Per-article digital product revenue: $0–$50  (top of funnel, 0.1–1% conversion)
Total per-article revenue:          $1–$55
```

The wide band is the digital-product backend. Without the product, an article earns $0.20–$5 lifetime. With the product, an article can earn $50+. The product is the unit-economics lever that turns pageviews into dollars.

## Why this is a content-pipeline business

The structural argument is the cadence, not the AI quality. An operator who ships 540 articles at any production cost (human, AI, outsourced) has a $30K business. The AI compresses the per-article production cost to $1–$5, which is what makes the math work. Without AI, the same cadence costs $50–$200/article, which breaks the unit economics.

The pipeline shape:

```
SEO keyword research (daily, 1 hour)
   ↓
Article title + outline (AI-assisted, 10 min)
   ↓
Article draft (AI-generated, 5 min)
   ↓
Human editing (30–60 min)
   ↓
SEO optimization (title, meta, internal links, 15 min)
   ↓
Publish to Medium (1 min)
   ↓
Cross-post to personal site / LinkedIn (15 min)
   ↓
Index in Google Search Console (passive)
   ↓
Track pageviews + product conversion (passive)
```

The operator time per article: 1.5–2.5 hours. The cadence of 2 articles/day is sustainable solo with 3–5 hours of focused work. The compounding volume is the moat.

## The digital product backend

```
Article pageview → landing page → product offer
                                        ↓
                              e-book ($30–$100)
                              template pack ($50–$200)
                              mini-course ($100–$300)
                              consulting call ($200–$500)
```

The article is the top of funnel. The product is the conversion. Most articles convert at 0.1–1% of pageviews to product sales; the average order value is $30–$300. The product funnel is what turns pageviews into dollars. Without the product, the articles are a Medium-partner-program income ($0.20–$5 per article lifetime), which is not a business.

## Why the $55/article is misleading

The $30K across 540 articles is the average. The median is likely $0–$5, with a small number of high-performers driving the average. The 80/20 rule applies: ~20% of the articles (the high-traffic SEO wins) drive 80% of the revenue. The other 80% of the articles are content-farm filler that exists to support the long-tail SEO.

The structural argument is: shipping 540 articles is a volume game. The AI + SEO + product funnel combination makes the volume game profitable. The median article is a loss leader; the distribution is the win.

## What the case does not cover

Four gaps.

1. **Production cost.** The case does not name the per-article cost. The implied range is $1–$5.
2. **Median article revenue.** The $30K average is across 540 articles; the median is likely $0–$5. The average is driven by a small number of high-performers.
3. **Churn on the digital product.** The case does not give refund / chargeback rate. Digital products see 5–15% refund rates on a bad funnel.
4. **Sustainability.** The case does not address what happens in month 12–24 when Google's algorithm compresses the SEO traffic. The 'Helpful Content' updates have been deprioritizing thin AI content since 2024; the case does not address the long-term survival of the model.

## Take-away

The case is not 'AI content works'. The case is: AI + SEO + a digital product backend is a content-pipeline business, where the AI compresses the production cost, the SEO compresses the distribution cost, and the product funnel is the unit-economics lever. The $55/article average is misleading; the median is $0–$5. The model works because the volume compounds; the model is fragile because Google's algorithm is the silent partner.

For most operators reading this case, the bottom line is: stop looking for AI content magic. Build a content pipeline: SEO keyword research → AI draft → human edit → SEO optimize → publish → product funnel. The cadence is 1–2 articles per day. The product backend is what turns pageviews into dollars. The 9-month compounding is the moat.

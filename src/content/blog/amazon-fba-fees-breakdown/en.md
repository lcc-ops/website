faq:
  - q: 'How much does Amazon FBA cost per unit?'
    a: 'For a $25 small-standard product, expect $3.86 FBA fulfillment + 15% referral ($3.75) = $7.61 in Amazon fees. Add storage, aged-inventory surcharges, returns processing, and removal fees, and the total can reach 30–40% of the sale price.'
  - q: 'What is the Amazon FBA referral fee?'
    a: 'A percentage of the sale price, taken on every unit. Most categories charge 15%, but Amazon Device Accessories is 45%, Toys & Games 15%, Books 15%, and Beauty 8% on the portion over $10.'
  - q: 'What is the aged inventory surcharge on Amazon FBA?'
    a: 'A monthly fee for units sitting in a fulfillment center for more than 181 days. Small-standard items: $0.30/unit after 181 days, $0.60 after 271, $0.90 after 365. Long-term storage fees apply after 365 days.'
  - q: 'Do I pay FBA fees on returns?'
    a: 'Yes. Amazon keeps the referral fee on returned units and charges a returns processing fee equal to 100% of the original FBA fulfillment fee for items in certain categories.'
  - q: 'Is FBA still worth it in 2026?'
    a: 'For most products, yes — Prime visibility, free shipping, and easy returns convert 20–40% better than FBM. The math breaks for slow-moving inventory, oversized items, or products with returns above 10%.'

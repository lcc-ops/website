Amazon's fee schedule is 14 pages long, and most sellers price by the referral fee alone. The rest hides in fulfillment, storage, returns, and aged-inventory surcharges. Here is the full bill for a typical $25 product.

## The full fee stack on a $25 product

| Fee | Amount | % of price |
|---|---|---|
| **Sale price** | **$25.00** | **100%** |
| Referral fee (15%) | $3.75 | 15% |
| FBA fulfillment (small standard, ≤6 oz) | $3.06 | 12.2% |
| FBA fulfillment (small standard, 6–12 oz) | $3.43 | 13.7% |
| FBA fulfillment (small standard, 12–16 oz) | $3.86 | 15.4% |
| Closing fee (Books, Media, Video Games only) | $0.00 | 0% |
| **Amazon's cut before storage** | **$7.61** | **30.4%** |

That is the floor. Now add the surcharges most sellers forget.

## Storage fees

Amazon charges for the space your inventory occupies, measured in cubic feet per day. As of 2026, off-peak (Jan–Sep) storage is $0.87/cubic foot/month; peak (Oct–Dec) is $2.40/cubic foot/month.

For a $25 product in a 12×8×4 inch box (~0.222 cubic feet):

- Off-peak monthly: $0.19
- Peak monthly: $0.53

Storage costs look small per unit but compound. A 1,000-unit run that takes 6 months to sell eats roughly $0.10/unit per month off-peak, or $600 total.

## Aged inventory surcharge

Units sitting in FBA more than 181 days get hit with a monthly surcharge:

| Days in FBA | Small-standard surcharge |
|---|---|
| 0–180 | $0 |
| 181–270 | $0.30/unit/month |
| 271–330 | $0.60/unit/month |
| 331–365 | $0.90/unit/month |
| 365+ | Long-term storage fee: $6.90/cubic foot (or $0.15/unit, whichever is higher) |

A unit that takes 9 months to clear is paying $1.80 in aged-inventory surcharges alone — on top of storage.

## Returns processing

Amazon keeps the referral fee on returned units, and adds a returns processing fee for items in certain categories (apparel, shoes, watches, jewelry, luggage, and most consumer goods).

The returns processing fee is the lesser of:

- 100% of the original FBA fulfillment fee, or
- $5.00

For our $25 product with a $3.86 fulfillment fee, every return costs Amazon nothing and costs you $3.86 plus the referral fee. With a 10% return rate, returns wipe $0.77 per unit sold on average.

## Removal and disposal

If you decide to clear out dead inventory:

- Removal order: $0.75/unit (standard) or $1.50/unit (oversize)
- Disposal order: $0.30/unit (standard) or $0.60/unit (oversize)

For 500 units of dead stock, removal runs $375. Some sellers use this as the cost of a wrong bet.

## Worked example: pricing for 30% net margin

Goal: keep 30% net after every Amazon fee and your cost of goods.

For a $25 small-standard product with 8% returns, 6-month storage, and $6 landed cost:

```
net = 0.30
referral = 0.15
fulfillment = 3.86
returns_loss = 0.08 × (3.86 + 3.75) = 0.61
storage = 0.40
surcharges = 0.50

price = (cost + fulfillment + storage + surcharges + returns_loss) ÷ (1 − referral − net)
price = (6 + 3.86 + 0.40 + 0.50 + 0.61) ÷ (1 − 0.15 − 0.30)
price = 11.37 ÷ 0.55
price = $20.67
```

At $20.67, every fee and the 8% return rate are absorbed; net margin is 30%. Anything below that price is a loss on every return-heavy month.

## What changes the math most

| Lever | Impact per $25 product |
|---|---|
| Return rate going from 5% to 10% | −$0.38/unit |
| Aged inventory (181+ days) | −$0.30/unit/month |
| Switching to FBM (you ship) | saves $3.86, loses ~20% conversion |
| Multi-channel fulfillment (MCF) | adds $0.50–$1.50/unit |
| Adding a $5 coupon | reduces referral by $0.75, but cuts margin $4.25 |

## FBA vs FBM — when to switch

FBA wins when:

- Your product is small, light, and conversion-sensitive
- You want Prime visibility
- Your return rate is below 8%

FBM wins when:

- Product is oversized, slow-moving, or has a return rate above 12%
- You can ship cheaper than $3.86/unit from your own warehouse
- Storage surcharges are eating your margin
